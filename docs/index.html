Abstract

Artificial intelligence holds significant promise for enhancing clinical decision support, yet existing systems often struggle with inflexibility, limited interpretability, and poor adaptability to new tasks or data modalitiesâ€”challenges that are especially pronounced in ophthalmology due to its reliance on multimodal imaging. We present EyeAgent, the first interpretable, multimodal agent framework for comprehensive clinical support in ophthalmology. Powered by a large language model (DeepSeek-V3), EyeAgent dynamically orchestrates 50 specialized ophthalmic tools to support a broad range of tasks including classification, segmentation, generation, and quantitative analysis, supporting 23 imaging modalities. Its modular architecture enables adaptive multimodal reasoning and transparent, step-by-step outputs without retraining, even as data types evolve. EyeAgent also handles complex scenarios such as hierarchical diagnoses and conflict resolution, outperforming GPT-4o and complementing human expertise. In an external validation on 200 real-world clinical cases simulating the decision-making logic of expert clinicians, EyeAgent achieved 93.7% tool selection accuracy, with expert-rated acceptability scores of 88.0% for accuracy, 93.0% for completeness, 90.5% for safety, 94.0% for reasoning, and 88.5% for interpretability. These results demonstrate EyeAgent's potential as a flexible, explainable, and extensible AI framework for ophthalmology and broader medical applications.
